1. Design & Creativity (24/08/2018 - 01/11/2018)

		            
Creativity is a crucial ingredient of design. In this course I had explored what creativity meant for the design process and how I could use creativity in this process. And since visualizing ideas, concepts and messages is an important skill to enhance my creativity and to cooperate with others in design processes, a significant part of the course has helped me to focus on developing my visualizing skills.

2. Introduction to UX (24/08/2018 - 29/10/2018)

		            
This course is about knowing what you want to achieve when designing for User Experience. The main focus of this course helped me to develop a frame of reference that can be used to take a stand when discussing, evaluating and designing User Experience. I had learnt to gain insight into what User Experience was and what factors to take into account when creating a great User Experience after the course.
It's all about Usability, Engagement, Meaning and Usefulness.

3. Research for Design I (24/08/2018 - 30/10/2018)

		            
UX designers are investigative designers. In order to gain the curiosity effectively spark the inspiration and to make founded choices in the design processes, I had learnt to build an understanding of research and to develop my research skills. In this course, I focused on basics of research by example of the method of User Trialling, in which observation and interviewing were combined to learn about users’ interactions with technology in general and the RijkMuseum website itself.

4. Intercultural Competence (26/08/2018 - 28/10/2018)

		            
This course enhanced the awareness and purpose in the acquirement of attitude, knowledge and skills that enable me to behave in a way that is appropriate and communicate effectively when dealing with other cultures. 6. Programming Boothcamp (02/11/2018 - 08/11/2018)

		            
Programming is an essential skill during each part of the design process. It is used alongside other techniques, to stimulate creativity, validate design solutions and communicate resulting products. This introductory course taught me knowledge and understanding of the logic and structure required in programming using Processing. Though it did not aim to teach programming for any single production environment or specific object (e.g. front or backend development, smartphone), I still learnt a lot from the lecturers with basic programming and created things I never thought I could do.
